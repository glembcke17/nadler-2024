What does Jerrold Nadler stand for?

Here are the Democratic Representative’s stances on immigrants, Eric Adams’s indictment, former president Donald Trump, and more.

By: Gretchen Lembcke Peña

October 13, 2024

With the primary elections right around the corner, it is time for people to decide who they plan on voting for. Although voting for the presidential candidate is crucial, when it comes to the future of their communities, local representatives are the ones who make the ultimate decisions that could affect them directly, such as “adopting local laws and having a link in the relationship between states and federal government under the federal system” according to the NY State website.

In District 12, also known as Washington Heights and Inwood, Democratic Representative Jerrold “Jerry” Nadler has advocated for the lives of underrepresented communities throughout his 30 years in Congress. However, what exactly does he stand for? Here are key things you should know about him and his stances before November 5th.

He has always been an advocate for immigrants

According to his website, Nadler states: “This is a country of immigrants. It’s past time our laws—and our rhetoric—reflect that fundamental fact”. As a display of this statement, he has been an ongoing supporter of the DREAM Act , which provides protections to undocumented immigrants. He also helped fund President Biden’s Build Back Better Framework , which provides funding for education, combating climate change, expanding affordable healthcare, and bringing down costs to strengthen the middle class.

Other than having a real-estate feud with former president Donald Trump in the 1980s, Nadler has continuously opposed the Republican’s views on immigration. Following two mass shootings in Texas, when Trump commented on doing background checks on gun owners with immigration reform, Nadler was quick to comment on this. “What’s the connection between background checks for guns and immigration reform? That we have to keep guns out of the hands of the invading hordes, of less-than-human people coming across our borders? That’s the implication”.

He has called for Eric Adams to resign.

After Mayor Eric Adams was charged with bribery and campaign finance offenses, Nadler quickly voiced his opinion on Twitter. The Democratic Representative took to Twitter to release a statement:

He is a loyal ally of the LGBTQIA+ community

Nadler has been through thick and thin with the queer community. According to LGBTQIA+ publication The Advocate, Jerry “is one of the nation’s fiercest protectors of LGBT rights and a powerful ally to trans equality”. Nadler's website also states that he was the first person in Congress to support marriage equality.

To add on, he himself wrote the Respect for Marriage Act, Uniting American Families Act, Father Mychal Judge Act, and Equal Access to Social Security Act . These all deliver the same rights a heterosexual individual may have to the LGBTQ+ community. He has even participated in pride parades in the past as a display of support.

He believes healthcare should be accessible to everyone.

Other than being the original cosponsor of the Medicare-for-All Act, he has been fighting to make it law. He has also created a landmark bill, The Pregnant Workers Fairness Act, which protects the health of pregnant individuals in the workplace. He stands for the right to accessible birth control, has introduced legislation for lower-cost prescriptions, and has always been vocal about his belief in vaccines and science to uphold the health and safety of the American people.
